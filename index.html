Hi, I’m Freddie! I’m looking to get onto a coding bootcamp because I see my future in tech, and I want to get onto the job market in London. And I want to apply here because I feel that Founders and Coders is the future of learning: peer-led, democratic and all about community.

And for me, the biggest strength of Founders and Coders is the diversity of that community. Sadly, systemic discrimination affects all walks of life, and the tech world in particular. By this I mean repeated acts of discrimination, intentional or not, against certain identities (such as race, class and gender), throughout institutions and society as a whole. This is compounded by the effects of socioeconomic inequality.

All too often these acts of discrimination go unnoticed, unable to be pinned down. I wanted to write a webpage that visualises the system to underline just how important the work that Founders and Coders is doing is.

The System

A child brought up in a working class family will hear 4 million fewer words by age 3 than a child brought up in a wealthy family.

Between the ages of 4 and 11, £50,000 more is spent on a privately educated pupil than a state educated pupil.

A student on free school meals is 27% less likely to achieve five or more GCSEs at grades A* to C than the average student.

Private school students are 55 times more likely to win a place at Oxbridge than free school meal students.

A privately educated student is:

  • 4 times more likely to become an MP
  • 6 times more likely to become a high-paid BBC star
  • 8 times more likely to become a top doctor
  • 10 times more likely to become a senior judge

    A black schoolboy in England is around three times more likely to be permanently excluded compared to the whole school population.

    At the age of eleven, when he is preparing to take his SATs, research indicates that he will be systematically marked down by his own teachers.

    72% of white students who enter university with three 'B' grade A-levels attain a 2.1 degree or above, compared to 53% of black students.

    Almost 70 per cent of the professors teaching in British universities are white men.

    Applicants with white-sounding names are called to interview far more often that those with African- or Asian- sounding names.

    “It’s always a boy – there’s little to no research in this area focused on the life chances of black girls”

    Among those who have achieved grade A or A* in GCSE physics, just 13% of girls take physics at A-level, compared to 39% of boys.

    But girls are almost two and half times more likely to take physics at A-level if they are at a single-sex school.

    90% of engineering and physical sciences funding in UK goes to male-led projects.

    Up to 30,000 women are sacked each year simply for being pregnant. Each year an estimated 440,000 women lose out on pay or promotion as a result of pregnancy.

    At the current rate of progress, it will take 280 years to close the gender gap in computer science.
